Strategic Conclusions — CFO Summary
Prepared June 2026 · Based on SmartBuilder actuals FY2023–FY2026 + Budget Model
EMS Mining was a profitable business through FY2024 (R195M+ revenue, positive EBITDA). Revenue collapsed as mining contracts ended without replacement — from R253M (FY2023) to zero by November 2025. The defining event was not the loss of contracts alone: PAYE and VAT were not remitted to SARS for years as cash was diverted to operations, creating a R64.5M statutory liability that SARS can enforce by account freeze — without a court order. This is the gating factor for all recovery scenarios. The business model is not fundamentally broken: the budget shows R59.9M revenue and positive EBITDA from October 2026 on signed contracts. But contracts alone do not save the company if SARS intercepts collections.
🔴 Critical — Do First
1. SARS Section 167 agreement
Prerequisite to everything. Without a payment arrangement, any AR collection (R12.5M) can be immediately attached. All new contract revenue is at risk.

2. Confirm FY2027 contracts
Budget assumes 5 contracts start Aug 2026. These must be signed, not pipeline. Delay of 1 month = –R5.7M revenue, extended cash burn.
🟡 High — Do After SARS Deal
3. Collect AR R12.5M
Mota-Engil, Black Mountain and others owe money for work already done. Must be collected only after Section 167 is in place — otherwise SARS attaches it.

4. Resolve legal AP (R15.0M)
Tswelopele R5.4M + Sandvik R4.8M are in legal proceedings. Unresolved — these creditors can obtain judgment and attach assets (including rigs needed for new contracts).
🟢 Possible — Execute FY2027
5. Mobilise equipment Aug 2026
Drill rigs currently idle + depreciating. Budget contracts require mobilisation by Aug 2026. Check which rigs are operationally fit and which are under asset finance liens.

6. EBITDA breakeven: October 2026
From Oct '26 onwards, R5.7M/month revenue exceeds cash costs. Company generates positive operating cash flow for first time since FY2024.

Labour & Site Costs — What's Included

Labour (GL 200xxx, 500000):
Salaries and wages — site crews (drillers, machine operators) and Head Office staff (management, finance, admin). PAYE deducted from payroll and supposed to be remitted to SARS monthly. In FY2026 with zero site activity, labour = skeleton HO cost only (~R1–1.8M/month).


Site Costs (GL 100xxx):
Direct operating expenses at the mine: rock tools & drill bits (GL 100400), repairs & maintenance (GL 100150), fuel, consumables, subcontracted services. These scale with production volume — when revenue dropped to zero, site costs dropped too. Negative values in some months = supplier credits/reversals.

Overheads & Depreciation — What's Included

Overheads (GL 400–499xxx, excl. 403xxx):
Head Office indirect costs: office rent, insurance, professional fees (legal, audit), bank charges, IT & telecoms. Budget: R1.97M/month fixed. Sep–Oct 2024 spike to R18–20M = believed to be SARS provisions accrued at year-end. This is the single biggest contributor to FY2025 losses.


Depreciation (GL 403xxx):
Non-cash charge for wear of drill rigs and mining equipment. Does not consume cash. FY2025: R37M depreciation on idle rigs. For cash management, use EBITDA (before depreciation), not Net Profit. Book loss of –R41M in FY2025 was a cash loss of only –R4M (EBITDA).

Key Cost Observations
SARS — the defining event
Sep–Oct 2024 overhead spike (R18M + R20M) = SARS tax provisions booked at transition year-end. PAYE arrears accumulated over years of cash stress. SARS can freeze accounts without court order — explains why recovery stalled despite some new revenue Jul–Nov 2024.
Labour: the sticky cost
In FY2026 with zero revenue, labour still R1.3–1.8M/month. These are HO staff costs — direct site labour drops to zero when sites close, but management/admin remain. Minimum cost floor to keep company alive: ~R1M/month.
Cash loss vs. book loss
Net loss FY2025 = –R41M. Cash loss (EBITDA) = –R4M. Gap = R37M non-cash depreciation on idle rigs. For liquidity decisions, EBITDA is the right metric. Rigs depreciating without income = destroyed value, not cash.
